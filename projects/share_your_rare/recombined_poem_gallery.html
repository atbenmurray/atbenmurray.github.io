--- layout: default title: Creative Complexity ---

Following on from the Genetic Alliance workshop, we wanted to show how peoples' individual poems were recombined and presented as shared experiences. The participants chose lines from the poems written by themselves and their fellow group members in the first phase of the workshop, and created new poems. Looking at the results, we can certainly see that certain lines of certain poems were strongly evocative or compelling to people, or were able to take on slightly altered meanings in their new context.

We thought of this process as being something like genetic recombination, and we were happy at the outcome. Not only did we see people recombining lines, but we also saw the poetic equivalent of replacements, insertions, deletions, translocations, and other 'poetic mutations'. We have presented these here in a manner that evokes the presentation of mutations in sequences of DNA.
